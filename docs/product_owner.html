MVP and OKRs

Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Description

The MVP for the IE Bank app will establish a foundational platform for digital banking, focusing on simplicity, security, and essential functionality. This version will cater to two primary user groups: bank administrators and individual customers, providing them with dedicated portals to manage their respective needs effectively.

Core Features

  • Admin Portal: Efficient User Management

    • The Admin Portal serves as the control center for bank administrators, allowing them to manage user accounts and ensure the smooth operation of the banking system. A default administrator account will be pre-configured during the application setup, offering secure initial access to the portal. Once logged in, the administrator can:

      • View User Accounts: Access a comprehensive list of registered bank users, displaying their associated bank accounts and basic details

      • Create New Users: Easily add new bank users by filling out their details and initializing their profiles with a secure password

      • Update User Information: Modify user credentials or update personal details as required

      • Delete User Accounts: Safely remove inactive or incorrect user profiles and their associated data

    • This portal ensures that the administrator has full control over the user database, enabling streamlined management through an intuitive interface

  • User Portal: Personalized Banking Experience

    • The User Portal is designed for individual customers to manage their bank accounts with ease. To onboard new users, the portal includes a simple and secure registration process. Users can create an account by providing a unique username, confirming their password, and completing basic form validations. Upon successful registration, the system automatically generates a new bank account with a random account number, ready for immediate use

    • After logging in, users gain access to a personalized dashboard that displays their associated bank accounts and recent transactions. The portal emphasizes user security, requiring credentials to be hashed and securely stored in the backend database

    • Core functionalities include:

      • Account Management: Users can view detailed information about their accounts, such as balances and transaction histories, ensuring transparency and control

      • Fund Transfers: Customers can seamlessly transfer money to other accounts within the bank by specifying the recipient’s account number and the transfer amount. The system ensures that the transfer amount does not exceed the account balance, safeguarding users from overdrafts

    • This portal delivers a straightforward banking experience, prioritizing usability and reliability while maintaining strict adherence to security standards

  • Secure Authentication and Data Storage

    • Security is at the heart of the MVP. Both admin and user portals feature a robust authentication system requiring valid credentials (username and password) for access. To protect user data:

      • Passwords are encrypted and stored as secure hashes in the PostgreSQL database

      • Advanced authentication methods, such as multi-factor authentication or OAuth, are deferred for future releases to keep the MVP lightweight and focused

  • Simple and Functional Interface

    • The MVP frontend will be developed using Vue.js, ensuring a reactive and smooth user experience. While visual aesthetics are not a priority at this stage, the interface will be clean and straightforward, allowing users to focus on functionality. Accessibility and responsiveness will be considered for future iterations

Scrum Methodology/DevOps Collaboration

Scrum Methodology

  • Implement Scrum Framework

    • Azure Boards Backlog Grooming: Create a prioritized product backlog with epics broken down into user stories, and acceptance criteria for the MVP. Ensure that the backlog is refined and updated regularly to reflect the team's progress and priorities. Assign story points to user stories to estimate complexity and effort required for implementation.

    • Sprint Planning: Define sprint goals based on set requirements of MVP, as well as current project Roadmap. Select user stories from the product backlog and assign them to the sprint backlog. Ensure that the sprint backlog is achievable and well documented, as well as aligns with the sprint goals. Ensure that the team understands the scope and acceptance criteria for each user story.

    • Daily Standups: Conduct daily standup meetings to discuss progress, challenges, and plans for the day. Each team member should provide updates on their work, any blockers, and plans for the day. The Scrum Master should facilitate the meeting and ensure that the team stays focused and on track.

    • Sprint Review: At the end of each sprint, conduct a sprint review meeting to demonstrate the completed user stories to stakeholders and gather feedback. Review the sprint goals and discuss any changes or adjustments needed for the next sprint.

    • Retrospectives: Conduct retrospectives at the end of each sprint to reflect on the team's performance, identify areas for improvement, and plan action items for the next sprint. Encourage open communication and feedback from team members to foster continuous improvement.

  • Roles and Responsibilities

    • Product Owner: Responsible for defining and prioritizing the product backlog, ensuring that it is up-to-date and reflects the team's progress and priorities. Collaborates with stakeholders to gather requirements and feedback, and communicates the product vision and goals to the team.

    • Scrum Master: Facilitates the Scrum process, ensures that the team follows Scrum practices, and helps remove any impediments that may affect the team's progress.

    • Scrum Team: Responsible for implementing user stories, meeting sprint goals, and delivering a potentially shippable product increment at the end of each sprint.

The integration ensures that team members can easily join meetings and collaborate in real-time.
